Industrial Safety Guidelines

1. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
   - Always wear appropriate PPE including hard hats, safety glasses, gloves, and steel-toe boots in designated areas.
   - Ensure PPE is in good condition and replaced when damaged.

2. Machine Safety
   - Never operate machinery without proper training and authorization.
   - Always ensure safety guards are in place before starting equipment.
   - Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures must be followed when performing maintenance.

3. Fire Safety
   - Know the location of fire extinguishers and emergency exits.
   - Conduct regular fire drills to ensure all personnel are familiar with evacuation procedures.
   - Keep flammable materials stored in designated, secure areas.

4. Electrical Safety
   - Do not use electrical equipment with frayed cords or exposed wiring.
   - Keep work areas dry to prevent electrical shocks.
   - Only qualified electricians should perform electrical work.

5. Chemical Safety
   - Store chemicals in properly labeled containers.
   - Follow Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDS) for handling and storage instructions.
   - Use appropriate ventilation when working with hazardous chemicals.

6. Emergency Procedures
   - Report all accidents and near-misses immediately.
   - Know the emergency contact numbers for medical assistance and fire department.
   - Participate in safety training programs regularly.
